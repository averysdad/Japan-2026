Early April is peak sakura season. Check forecasts closer to the trip — bloom dates shift by a week or more depending on the year. Slot these in when timing is right.

Tokyo Sakura Spots

Cherry Blossoms
Best worked into Apr 4, 10, or 11 depending on peak bloom timing. All are near your existing routes.
  • Meguro River top pick
    You're already walking to Nakameguro on Apr 10 — the canal is lined with 800+ cherry trees, lit with pink lanterns at dusk. Time your Traveler's Factory visit for golden hour and walk the river afterward. Arguably the single best sakura moment in Tokyo. Evenings are magical but crowded on weekends.
  • Shinjuku Gyoen
    Tokyo's best all-around hanami park — 1,000+ trees across 65 varieties, meaning some will be blooming no matter your exact dates. Huge lawns perfect for a family picnic. ¥500 entry. No alcohol allowed, which keeps it calm and family-friendly. Could replace the afternoon gap on Apr 11 (during the spa split). Closes 6:00 PM. ~20 min taxi from Grand Hyatt.
  • Ueno Park
    Classic hanami atmosphere — over 1,000 trees lining the main path. More lively and festive than Shinjuku Gyoen (food stalls, crowds, energy). Could pair with a Nakano Broadway morning on Apr 12 if you leave slightly earlier. ~30 min from Grand Hyatt by metro.
  • Chidorigafuchi
    Moat-side walkway near the Imperial Palace — rent rowboats surrounded by overhanging cherry branches. Stunning for photos. Evening illumination until 10 PM. Could work as an after-dinner detour on Apr 10 or 11. ~15 min taxi from Roppongi.
  • Roppongi Hills / Mori Garden
    Right at your Grand Hyatt doorstep. Small but beautiful — illuminated cherry trees along the garden path behind Roppongi Hills. Zero detour needed. Perfect for a nightcap stroll on any evening Apr 9–11.

Osaka & Kyoto Sakura Spots

Cherry Blossoms
Best worked into Apr 5 evening, Apr 6 (Kyoto day), or Apr 7 morning. Osaka and Kyoto bloom at roughly the same time.
  • Osaka Castle Nishinomaru Garden top pick
    600+ cherry trees with the castle as backdrop — one of the most iconic sakura views in Japan. Evening illumination available. Already listed as an Apr 7 option (Option A). ¥350 entry. If you only see one Osaka sakura spot, make it this one.
  • Kema Sakuranomiya Park
    4,500+ trees along the Okawa River — walkable from Caption by Hyatt Namba (~20 min along the river). Perfect for an early morning or evening stroll on Apr 5 or 6. Less touristy than the castle. River cruise boats run during peak bloom.
  • Philosopher's Path, Kyoto
    Canal-side walking path lined with hundreds of cherry trees — 2km from Ginkaku-ji to Nanzen-ji. Not on your Apr 6 route but could replace the Gion afternoon wander if blossoms are peaking. ~20 min taxi from Higashiyama.
  • Maruyama Park, Kyoto
    Adjacent to Yasaka Shrine — you're walking right past it on Apr 6. The famous weeping cherry tree (shidarezakura) is illuminated at night. Free entry. Even a 15-minute detour through the park is worth it during bloom.
  • Arashiyama area
    The mountains around Togetsukyo Bridge turn pink in early April — you'll already be here on Apr 6 morning. Stand on the bridge and look upstream for the classic postcard view. No detour needed, just be aware of it.

🍸 Ginza Bars — Post-Disney Adults Night Out
For after the parks close at 9pm on Apr 8 or 9. Maihama → Tokyo Station by Keiyo Line (~17 min, runs until midnight) → taxi or walk to Ginza (~10 min). Return by midnight for an early park start. Best night: Apr 9 (Thu) — you finish DisneySea with a proper sit-down dinner at Canaletto, exiting in better shape than after a full walking day at Disneyland.

Bar High Five

World's 50 Best · Cocktail
B1F, Efflore Ginza 5 Building · 4-4-9 Ginza · Open until 2am
  • Master bartender Hidetsugu Ueno — the most famous cocktail bar in Japan, perennial World's 50 Best Bars. No menu: tell him what spirits or flavours you're in the mood for and he builds something perfectly calibrated to you. Known for the hard shake, hand-carved diamond ice, and his signature Full Blossom (gin, sakura liqueur, maraschino). 10 bar stools + two small tables — arrive before 8pm to guarantee a seat, or later in the week when it quiets down.
    Budget ¥2,500–4,000pp (drinks + seat charge). Worth every yen. If you visit one bar in Ginza, make it this one.

Star Bar Ginza

Legend · Whisky + Classics
B1F, Sanwa Ginza Building · 1-5-13 Ginza · open late
  • Owner Hisashi Kishi is one of the great Japanese bartenders — has trained a generation of the city's best mixologists. Perfected classic cocktails, exceptional Japanese and Scotch whisky selection, hand-carved ice on everything. Very small and serious. No ordering from a menu — the conversation with the bartender is the experience.
    Intimate, unhurried, world-class. Seat charge + ¥1,200+ drinks. Pairs beautifully with High Five if you want to do two bars in a night.

Punch Room

Asia's 50 Best · Relaxed
2F, The Tokyo Edition Ginza · 2-8-13 Ginza
  • Asia's 50 Best Bars. Specialises in punch cocktails reinvented with Japanese spirits and flavours — sakura, wasabi, daikon, togarashi combined in ways that genuinely surprise. Dark walnut walls and plush velvet seating evoking a 19th-century London gentlemen's club. More relaxed and social than the serious quiet bars — good music, chatter, a buzz on weeknights.
    The most approachable of the serious Ginza bars. Good if you want excellent cocktails without the reverent hush of High Five.

13F, Ginza Six · 6-10-1 Ginza · opens 5pm

Bar Landscape

Whisky · Classics
B1F, Sanwa Ginza Building · 6-4-9 Ginza
  • Run by husband-and-wife duo Kazuma and Tamiko Matsuo, both award-winning bartenders who trained under master Mori before opening their own place. Warm, atmospherically lit, excellent Japanese whisky list. Standout cocktails: whisky sour, tomato martini, passion fruit cocktail. A refreshing change from the typically all-male bartender scene.
    Slightly more intimate and warm than the uber-serious bars. Budget ¥2,000–3,500pp.

Beer Hall Lion

Classic · Casual · Budget
5-chome Ginza · open until ~11pm
  • Sapporo's historic beer hall, open since 1934. High ceilings, tiled mosaic walls, great draft beer, German-style food. Loud and lively — the complete opposite of the hushed whisky bars. Much cheaper (no seat charge, beer from ¥800), great atmosphere, and a genuine piece of Ginza history. Good if you just want a cold beer and some food after a long day.
    No cover charge, no reverence required. The anti-High Five. Both have their place depending on the night.
